E-LINK 2.0


OVERVIEW


E-Link is a data repository for scientific and technical information (STI) sponsored by the Department of Energy (DOE).


"E-Link facilitates the electronic submittal of STI between DOE and its client community including researchers, reviewers, research administrators, and others doing business with DOE. STI deliverables provided to DOE through E-Link are announced, as appropriate, on web products maintained by OSTI, which provide free and convenient public access to full text and bibliographic data of products resulting from DOE-funded research." - Source: E-LINK



PROJECT DETAILS


  • Client: Department of Energy (DOE) - Office of Scientific and Technical Information
  • Role: UX/UI Design Intern
  • Team: Applications team members and backend developers
  • Timeline: Summer 2023


THE PROBLEM


Through conversations with three active E-LINK users, it became clear that:

  • The platform was outdated, having been created over two decades ago
  • It lagged behind contemporary data repositories
  • The system had become difficult to work with, leading to inefficient workflows


RESEARCH


User Groups

  • Primary: Information Systems (IS) team - responsible for curation and enhancement
  • Secondary: Lab members - submitting scientific and technical information (STI)

Benchmark Analysis

Comparison with alternative repositories revealed:

  • Modern repositories offer user-centric browsing experiences
  • Industry standard features include mandatory user authentication
  • Well-structured submission categories and consistent processes


DESIGN PROCESS


Initial Exploration


Design priorities:

  • Simplify submission and curation processes
  • Create consistent user authentication flow
  • Modernize the interface while maintaining familiarity

Design Decisions

  • Implemented simplified color palette based on blue
  • Used Roboto font family for consistency with industry standards
  • Streamlined information architecture
  • Optimized search functionality for OSTI number lookups
